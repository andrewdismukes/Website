Course Description

Welcome to HDFS 102. Individuals and families are extremely diverse, complex, and fascinating. This course provides an overview of life-span developmental tasks (physical, cognitive, language, social, emotional) examined from various theoretical perspectives. We will discuss topics related to family diversity, individual and family health and well-being, and reciprocal relationships. We will examine individual and family life theories, learn about human and family development as life-long, multidimensional processes, and make practical applications of contemporary research and course content.

Student Learning Outcomes

  • demonstrate knowledge of human development within the diverse social contexts of family, community, society, and culture.

  • observe and document effects of major influences (physical, behavioral, socioemotional, cognitive) on the development of the individual within the family as they occur across the life span.

  • demonstrate knowledge of the impact of structure, functioning, and sequencing in the development of families across the life span.

  • effectively communicate an understanding of the process of change and the impact of decision-making on individual and family development.

  • apply critical thinking skills to evaluate contemporary individual and family developmental issues.

Student Learning Outcomes

  • develop appreciation and exhibit respect for diversity, continuity, and adaptation as they relate to social justice issues within individual and family life development.

  • reflect upon your own individual and family life development in relation to our course content.

  • enhance your personal and professional lives by applying your knowledge of individual and family life development to your future experiences.

Class Structure

Overview

Each week, you will watch a video that introduces the course content. This video will be posted by Wednesday morning of each week and will be hosted on the course content website HERE. I will extend the book material in these videos, so it is critical that you read the chapter before watching the video - this will be an extension, not a summary of the chapter. You will submit questions from each weekly reading that you would like me to answer by Monday morning of the relevant week (see below). Furthermore, embedded in these videos will be quiz questions for you to answer. These questions will come up intermittently during the videos. I suggest that you pause the video and jot don your answer as you watch. You will submit your answers via blackboard to the weekly quizzes tab by Friday.

Weekly Student Questions

I expect you to read every week. This is an excellent textbook and a wonderful learning resource. To this end, each week via blackboard, I expect you to submit two questions that emerged during your weekly readings. This is worth 15 percent of your final grade. I will review these questions each week and look for common themes or patterns that stand out, and try to address that in the weekly video. If you ask a particularly insightful question and it stands out I may choose to award a bonus point for that question. You will submit these questions through the weekly folders in blackboard. These are due Monday at 8 am.

Weekly Quizzes

Each week I will ask a series of quiz questions during the weekly video. I suggest you pause your video and mark your answer. These will be multiple choice or short answer. I will ask these quiz questions randomly during the weekly video, and I will not provide the question on blackboard. This is to encourage you to review the video each week, and to highlight content I think is especially important. After you have written your 3-5 answers down, you can go to blackboard and submit your answers into the “Quiz Questions” assignment of the relevant week folder. This must be done by Friday at 11 pm.

Assignments

You will have three assignments over the course of this semester. Assignments one and three will be personal reflections. Assignment two will be a child observation. All of these assignments will be uploaded via blackboard. Individual assignment instructions will be posted separately.

Final Exam

Your final exam will be based on interview(s) you conduct, and reflection on what you learn in those interview(s). Final Exam instructions are posted separately.
